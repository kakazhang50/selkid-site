Children are already meeting AI-shaped tools. The gap is not "how to prompt" — it is **how examples become answers**.

In **Training Data**, Rex and Dino follow a Smart Trail that only learned from rabbit footprints. The story is funny, concrete, and fair: the tool is not evil, it is **limited by what it was shown**.

## Classroom moves that work

- Ask: *What did the Smart Trail actually learn from?*
- Sort real vs. missing examples (activity in the unit).
- Close with: *When should a person check, not just trust the fast answer?*

That last line is the bridge from SEL habits (pause, notice, repair) to AI-age judgment.
